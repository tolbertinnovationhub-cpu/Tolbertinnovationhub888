TOEFL Note-Taking Guide

Effective note-taking tips:
- Use short phrases and keywords, not full sentences.
- Abbreviate common words (e.g., "govt" for government, "eg" for example).
- Use arrows and symbols to show relationships (→, ↑, ↓).
- Note transitions and signal words: however, therefore, for example, in contrast.
- Keep separate columns for main idea / supporting detail / example when possible.

Practice:
Listen to short academic lectures and practice capturing 4–6 key points with brief notes.
