Open Learning Hub: TOEFL Preparation Course

Prepare effectively for the TOEFL exam with focused skill-building and proven exam strategies.

Course Summary

This TOEFL Preparation Course combines language development with practical test strategies to help learners understand instructions, manage time, and respond confidently. Practice activities reflect common TOEFL question formats to simulate the real test experience.

Module 1 — Listening Comprehension

Lesson 1 — Understanding the TOEFL Listening Section

The TOEFL Listening section includes academic lectures and campus conversations that test your ability to understand spoken English in university settings.

Key Skills

  • Identifying main ideas
  • Understanding supporting details
  • Recognizing speaker attitude
  • Taking effective notes

Listening Strategy

  • Focus on the speaker's main message
  • Write short notes instead of full sentences
  • Pay attention to transitions like however, therefore, and for example

Practice Listening Activity

Listen to a short academic lecture about environmental science.

  1. What is the main topic of the lecture?
  2. What example does the speaker provide?
  3. What conclusion does the speaker make?

Module 1 Quiz

  1. What type of recordings appear in the TOEFL listening test?
  2. Why is note-taking important during listening?
  3. What should you listen for to identify the main idea?

Module 2 — Reading Analysis

Build skills to analyze academic passages, understand vocabulary in context, and answer evidence-based reading questions.

Lesson 1 — Understanding TOEFL Reading

The TOEFL Reading section contains academic passages similar to university textbooks.

Reading Skills

  • Skimming — Quickly read to understand the main idea.
  • Scanning — Look for specific information such as names, dates, or key facts.
  • Vocabulary in Context — Determine meaning based on surrounding sentences.

Practice Reading Passage

Online education has become an important part of modern academic systems. Many universities now provide online courses that allow students to access lectures and materials from any location. This approach helps learners manage their time and continue their education while working.
  1. What is the main benefit of online learning mentioned in the passage?
  2. How do universities support online learning?
  3. Why do many students choose online courses?

Module 2 Quiz

  1. What is the purpose of skimming?
  2. What is scanning used for?
  3. How can context help understand vocabulary?

Module 3 — Integrated Writing

Learn to organize ideas from reading and listening sources into clear, structured written responses.

Lesson 1 — Understanding Integrated Writing

In the TOEFL Integrated Writing task, students read a short passage and listen to a lecture, then write a response explaining the relationship between the two sources.

Writing Structure

  • Introduction
  • Summary of the reading
  • Explanation of the lecture
  • Comparison between the two sources

Example Writing Task: Reading Topic: Renewable Energy Solutions. Lecture: Challenges of implementing renewable energy systems. Write a response explaining how the lecture relates to the reading.

Writing Tips

  • Take notes while reading and listening
  • Focus on relationships between ideas
  • Use clear paragraphs
  • Avoid personal opinions in integrated tasks

Practice Activity: Write a 150–225 word response summarizing the relationship between the reading and lecture.

Module 3 Quiz

  1. What two sources are used in the integrated writing task?
  2. Should personal opinions be included in integrated writing?
  3. What is the recommended word range?

Module 4 — Speaking Responses

Improve fluency, pronunciation, and response organization for both independent and integrated speaking tasks.

Lesson 1 — TOEFL Speaking Format

The speaking section includes independent and integrated tasks.

Independent Speaking

Students give personal opinions on familiar topics.

Integrated Speaking

Students combine reading, listening, and speaking to explain ideas.

Speaking Task Example

Question: Do you prefer studying alone or studying in groups? Explain your answer. Students have 15 seconds to prepare and 45 seconds to speak.

Speaking Strategy

  • Start with a clear opinion
  • Give two supporting reasons
  • Provide an example
  • Speak clearly and confidently

Practice Activity

Record your answer to: Describe a teacher who helped you learn something important. Speak for 45 seconds.

Module 4 Quiz

  1. How long do students usually have to prepare for speaking responses?
  2. What should be included in a strong speaking answer?
  3. Why is pronunciation important in the TOEFL speaking test?

Practice Activities & Mock Test

Throughout the course, students should complete listening comprehension exercises, reading analysis activities, writing assignments, and speaking practice recordings.

At the end of the course, students should complete a full TOEFL practice test that simulates the real exam environment.
