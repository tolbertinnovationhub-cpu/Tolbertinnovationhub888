IELTS Preparation Course

Complete learning materials to improve Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking for higher IELTS band scores.

Course Overview

This IELTS preparation program helps students improve skills in Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking through guided lessons, practical exercises, and mock practice that simulate the real IELTS test experience.

Recommended order: Listening → Reading → Writing → Speaking → Mock Test → Certificate.

Module 1 — Listening Practice

Lesson 1 — Understanding the IELTS Listening Test

The IELTS Listening test contains four sections with increasing difficulty. Each section includes a conversation or monologue.

Key Skills

  • Listening for main ideas
  • Identifying specific details
  • Recognizing different accents
  • Understanding instructions

Practice Exercise

Listen to a short conversation and answer the questions.

Note: Upload your practice audio to audio/listening1.mp3 or replace the src with your file.

Download Listening Answer Key (TXT)

Show transcript (Booking a hotel room — sample)

Hotel Receptionist: Good afternoon, Cityview Hotel. How can I help you?

Customer: Hello. I'd like to book a double room from Friday the 12th for three nights. Do you have availability?

Hotel Receptionist: Yes, we have a double room available. The rate is $90 per night, inclusive of breakfast.

Customer: That sounds good. Can I confirm the reservation under the name Jackson?

Hotel Receptionist: Certainly, Mr. Jackson. Your booking is confirmed for three nights, total cost $270. We'll email your confirmation shortly.

Listening Tips

  • Read questions before listening
  • Focus on keywords
  • Do not panic if you miss one answer
  • Write answers clearly

Module 2 — Reading Skills

Lesson 1 — Understanding the IELTS Reading Test

The IELTS reading test contains three passages with a total of 40 questions.

Reading Strategies

  • Skimming: Quickly read to understand the general idea.
  • Scanning: Search for specific information such as numbers or names.
  • Detailed Reading: Carefully read a paragraph to understand the meaning.

Module 2 Quiz

  1. What is skimming?
  2. What is scanning?
  3. How many reading passages are in the IELTS test?

Module 3 — Writing Task 1 & Task 2

Master essay structure, coherence, vocabulary use, and grammar control for both report and opinion-based writing tasks.

Lesson 1 — Writing Task 1 (Charts, Graphs, Tables)

Task 1 requires students to describe visual information clearly and objectively.

Basic Structure

  • Introduction
  • Overview
  • Body Paragraph 1
  • Body Paragraph 2

Lesson 2 — Writing Task 2 (Essay Writing)

Task 2 requires writing an essay of at least 250 words.

Writing Tips

  • Use clear paragraphs
  • Support ideas with examples
  • Avoid repeating vocabulary
  • Check grammar and spelling

Module 4 — Speaking Practice

Improve fluency and pronunciation through guided speaking drills, sample interview questions, and confidence-building responses.

Speaking Practice Activity

  • Record answers to cue-card prompts
  • Listen and self-evaluate pronunciation
  • Practice expanding responses with examples

Practice Activities & Mock Test

Students should complete listening comprehension exercises, reading analysis activities, writing assignments, and speaking practice recordings.

Final IELTS Mock Practice

  • Listening – 40 questions
  • Reading – 40 questions
  • Writing – Task 1 and Task 2
  • Speaking – simulated interview questions
